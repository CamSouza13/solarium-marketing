Case 01 · 2026

Pinebark Provisions — from regional to national.

Outfitter · Retail & gear
OutfitterRetail & gear

The engagement

A Vermont-based backcountry outfitter had the gear and the story but no way to reach climbers and backpackers outside New England. We rebuilt the brand, launched an algorithm-scored content engine, rewrote the site around conversion flows for their actual buyer, and stood up organic-first e-commerce that doesn't depend on paid spend to grow.

Twelve months later, organic traffic had more than tripled, repeat purchase rate nearly tripled, and the team was hiring its first dedicated in-house marketer — to manage what we'd set up, not replace it.

Case 02 · 2025

Deschutes Guide Co. — booking out seasons early.

Guide · Tour operator
GuideTour operator

The engagement

A 30-year-old rafting outfitter had a season-ending booking crunch every spring. Trips weren't filling until week-of, burning out the phones team, stressing the guides, and making it impossible to plan logistics. We built a reservations-aware content calendar, reworked trip pages around the actual decision process of a first-time rafter (not keyword stuffing), and targeted paid media at the exact window the customer was deciding.

The next full season sold through at 94% by opening day, ad spend dropped 38% on the same revenue, and the company booked its first-ever sold-out summer by early April.

The numbers

94%Season sellthrough
−38%Ad spend, same revenue
12 wkEarlier booking curve
Case 03 · 2025

Meadowrun Natives — wholesale pipeline in one season.

Grower · Native plants
GrowerNative plants

The engagement

A native-plant nursery wanted to move from retail walk-ins to a wholesale channel serving landscape architects and ecological restoration firms. They had the inventory and the growing expertise; what they lacked was a positioning story, a pricing narrative that held up against commodity plant brokers, and a way to get in front of the firms that could move real volume.

We built the positioning, the pricing one-pager, and a quiet outbound system rooted in horticultural credibility — not cold-call volume. One season later, $1.2M of pipeline from 18 landscape firms, three of which became recurring customers.

$1.2MNew wholesale pipeline
18Firms in first season
3Recurring partners
Case 04 · 2025

Ridge & Ridge Land Trust — donor growth after a plain-English rebuild.

Steward · Land trust
StewardLand trust

The engagement

A 200-member land trust had strong conservation outcomes, a volunteer board, and an annual report full of "stakeholders" and "mission-aligned partners." Donation page conversion was flat. We threw out every piece of copy that had come from a grant template and rewrote the site the way a landowner would actually describe the place.

The plain-English rewrite, paired with simple storytelling pages for three signature properties and a lightweight email sequence, grew the membership base 44% in one cycle and doubled the average recurring gift. The team also reclaimed 8 hours a week previously spent rewriting the same content for different audiences.

The numbers

+44%Members · one cycle
2×Avg. recurring gift
8 hrs/wkReclaimed for the team
Case 05 · 2024

Northwall Alpine — a sponsored athlete pool built from scratch.

Athlete · Gear brand
AthleteGear brand

The engagement

A four-person alpine-apparel brand wanted the brand halo that sponsored athletes give bigger competitors but couldn't afford it alone. We stood up a co-op athlete model before the co-op existed in name — a seven-climber roster funded and cross-promoted across three member brands, with a shared content kit and a single filmmaker on retainer.

Over 18 months the roster produced 44 pieces of usable content across the three brands, drove a 3.1× lift in branded search for Northwall specifically, and became the structural template for what we now run as the formal Solarium co-op athlete pool.

The numbers

7Climbers · 3 brands
44Content pieces · 18mo
3.1×Branded search lift
