Case 01 Fitness & Wellness

Voltra Fitness Co.:
From invisible to unavoidable.

Voltra Fitness Co. is a premium functional fitness studio on the West Coast. They had world-class coaches, stunning facilities, and content that looked incredible — but their social media reach had plateaued hard.

Their Instagram was averaging 200 impressions per post. Their TikToks were hitting the FYP just 8% of the time. They were creating five pieces of content a week and getting almost nothing in return.

The problem wasn't the content. The problem was that the content wasn't optimized for what each platform's algorithm was actually rewarding.

Key Results
0% Reach increase
$1.2M Revenue generated
67% TikTok FYP rate
14K New member signups
MONTHLY REACH TRAJECTORY — 12 MONTHS
The Challenge
  • Instagram reach plummeted 73% after algorithm shift to Reels-first ranking
  • TikTok FYP rate stuck at 8% despite high production value
  • $4K/month content budget producing minimal ROI
  • Competitors with worse content were outperforming them 3:1
Our Strategy
  • Built custom algorithm model for fitness content on IG + TikTok
  • Identified 6 "signal gaps" — watch time hooks, save triggers, and share mechanics they were missing
  • Restructured content calendar around algorithm-optimal posting cadence
  • Introduced "micro-authority" series — 15-sec tips that triggered binge-watch signals
The Results
  • 412% reach increase within 90 days
  • TikTok FYP rate jumped from 8% → 67%
  • Instagram Reels averaging 45K views (from 200)
  • 14,000 new member signups attributed to social
  • $1.2M in tracked revenue over 12 months
Cam didn't just tell us to 'post more Reels.' He showed us exactly why the algorithm was suppressing our content and built a system around the signals we were missing. Within six weeks, our reach was 4x what it had ever been. We've had to hire two new front desk staff to handle the volume.

Case 02 E-Commerce / DTC

Sunchasers Supply:
From ad spend hemorrhage to 3.1x ROAS.

Driftline is a direct-to-consumer outdoor lifestyle brand based in Southern California. Beautiful products, loyal community, strong brand identity — but their paid advertising strategy was bleeding money.

They were spending $28K/month on Meta and Google ads with a ROAS of 0.9x. That means they were losing money on every dollar spent. Their previous agency kept saying "give it time" while the ad platform algorithms kept deprioritizing their campaigns.

We modeled the auction dynamics of both platforms and discovered their entire bidding strategy was fighting the algorithm instead of working with it.

Key Results
3.1x ROAS (from 0.9x)
$1.8M Revenue in 6 months
-42% Cost per acquisition
156% Conversion rate lift
COST PER ACQUISITION — 12 MONTHS (↓ is better)
The Challenge
  • $28K/month ad spend with 0.9x ROAS — losing money
  • Meta's algorithm flagging their ads as low-relevance
  • Google Shopping campaigns stuck in "learning" limbo
  • Creative fatigue — audience was seeing the same ads 8+ times
Our Strategy
  • Modeled Meta and Google auction algorithms to find optimal bid/budget combinations
  • Built creative testing framework that aligned with Meta's "exploration" reward signals
  • Restructured campaign architecture to avoid audience fragmentation
  • Implemented organic-to-paid pipeline using top-performing social content as ad creative
The Results
  • ROAS jumped from 0.9x to 3.1x in 4 months
  • CPA dropped 42% while increasing total spend
  • $1.8M in tracked e-commerce revenue in 6 months
  • Conversion rate lifted 156% through landing page optimization
  • Now scaling to $60K/month profitably
Our old agency kept telling us to "trust the process." Cam showed us actual data on why our campaigns were underperforming — the algorithm was literally deprioritizing us because of how our campaigns were structured. He fixed it in two weeks. Our ROAS tripled in four months.

Trellix Insights:
Page nowhere to page one.

They were ranking on page 4–8 for their most important keywords. Competitors with thinner content were dominating page one. Their content was better but Google's algorithm wasn't rewarding it.

We ran our SEO algorithm model and found the answer in 48 hours: they were missing critical trust signals that Google's E-E-A-T framework now heavily weights.
