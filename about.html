◉ 01 — A note from the founder

We're running out of time to tell the story right.

The earth-based brands doing the most important work in the world right now — the land trusts, the regenerative growers, the small outfitters, the marine restoration nonprofits, the gear makers building things that last — are almost always the worst at marketing themselves. That's not an accident. It's a structural problem. And I built Solarium to solve it.

I started underwater. Reef restoration in Key Largo with the Coral Restoration Foundation. Marine plastic research in Waimanalo with the Center for Marine Debris Research. My job, in both places, was partly science and mostly translation — taking field data and turning it into something a donor, a policymaker, or a 14-year-old volunteer could actually understand and care about. I learned quickly that the quality of the science didn't matter if the story didn't land.

So I went to Harvard for data science. Not to leave the outdoors — to bring quantitative rigor to the story-telling problem. Audience modeling. Content scoring. Attribution. The math behind which stories reach which people, and why. Since 2024 I've been applying that at Redwood Group, building brand and messaging systems for creative and technology clients at scale.

Solarium Marketing is the shop that pulls all of it together. Marine-science credibility for brands protecting the outside. Harvard-trained data work that actually moves the algorithm. A plainspoken marketing practice that refuses to sound like marketing. No funnel-speak, no jargon, no campaigns that peak in week three and vanish in week four. We build systems that keep working.

The Earth Co-op is the sister vision. Big outdoor brands spend millions every year on sponsored athletes, gear testing, and retreats that make their marketing feel like a community. The brands I want to work with — the ones actually stewarding the land and water — can't afford that alone. A co-op is the version that works at their scale. Pool the athletes. Pool the gear. Pool the trips and the relationships. Build a network an order of magnitude larger than anything any single member could fund on their own. Free for our clients. Open to the public for the brands that just want in on the network.

There is a version of the next twenty years where the outdoor and conservation economy is loud, sophisticated, and ubiquitous in culture — because the brands at its core finally figured out how to tell their story at scale, together. That's what I'm building toward. If you're a brand whose work begins outside, I'd like to meet you. Usually on your land, your river, or your shop floor — before we write a word about you.

— Cam Cameron Souza · Founder, Solarium Marketing & The Earth Co-op
◉ 02 — How we work

Six tenets. We've turned down money over all of them.

01

Earn the place

Before we write about a landscape, a sport, or a species, we learn it. Show up. Put hands on it. Talk to the people who live with it. Most marketing copy in the outdoor world fails this test.

02

Outlive the campaign

A good brand, site, or story should still be earning attention in year three. We build accordingly — systems and assets over one-off stunts.

03

Plainspoken

No jargon. No funnel-speak. If a farmer or a field biologist wouldn't say it, we don't write it. This is harder than it sounds.

04

Protect trust

Trust and authenticity are the product in this industry. Anything that chips them is a loss, even if the numbers go up. We will tell you when we think you're wrong.

05

Small footprint

We think about the emissions and attention costs of the work we ship — tracking less, hosting greener, writing tighter, shooting local.

06

Senior-touch

No account managers between you and the work. Every project is touched by a senior — regardless of how many members join the co-op. That changes both the quality ceiling and the speed floor.
